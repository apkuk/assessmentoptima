# WorkStyle Compass v2 Research Basis

Status: Implementation-current research brief for the v2 assessment model.

## Executive Summary

WorkStyle Compass v2 is a developmental work-style assessment. It is designed to help people reflect on how they create value in complex work, how they coordinate with others, how they adapt under pressure, and how they use digital/AI tools with judgement.

The public theory is no longer benchmark-brand-led. The revised theory is:

> WorkStyle Compass measures how people turn complexity into contribution through three work operating systems: Operational Clarity, Human Coordination, and Adaptive Capacity.

This keeps the practical intent of serious occupational assessment while making the model more distinctive, ownable, and safer to explain publicly.

## Use Boundary

The instrument is research-informed, developmental, and exploratory. It must not be used for hiring, promotion, redundancy, compensation, diagnosis, performance rating, disciplinary decisions, or other high-stakes decisions about a person.

The right label is:

```text
developmental work-style assessment
```

Avoid:

```text
personality test
validated predictor
selection instrument
diagnostic profile
```

## Why The Model Changed

The previous model was original at item level, but it still resembled a familiar professional-assessment silhouette: execution, learning, influence, collaboration, emotional stability, strategy, integrity, change, and AI.

That overlap is scientifically useful because work-style constructs should relate to known behavioural science. But the public model needed a clearer centre of gravity. The v2 model frames work style as a pattern of value creation under complexity, coordination, and change.

## The 3x3 Work Operating System Model

| Work operating system | Domain | Core question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Operational Clarity | Commitment Rhythm | How do you convert intention into reliable progress? |
| Operational Clarity | Systems Sensemaking | How do you understand complexity, trade-offs, and downstream effects? |
| Operational Clarity | Augmented Judgement | How do you use AI/digital tools while preserving human accountability? |
| Human Coordination | Mobilising Communication | How do you create energy, relevance, and commitment through communication? |
| Human Coordination | Mutuality & Repair | How do you build collaboration, restore trust, and coordinate shared work? |
| Human Coordination | Trust Stewardship | How do you protect truth, fairness, humility, and decision integrity? |
| Adaptive Capacity | Adaptive Learning | How do you update your thinking when evidence, feedback, or context changes? |
| Adaptive Capacity | Pressure Regulation | How do you maintain perspective and choice under strain? |
| Adaptive Capacity | Change Navigation | How do you create movement through ambiguity without outrunning adoption? |

## Research Roots

The model is not a pure personality inventory. It blends trait-like tendencies, behavioural habits, leadership preferences, self-regulation patterns, values/ethics, and AI-era work practices.

That blend is acceptable for a developmental tool if the site is honest about it. The site should not claim the instrument has established norms, selection validity, clinical meaning, or predictive power.

### Big Five And Occupational Personality

Several domains should show conceptual overlap with established personality research:

- Commitment Rhythm relates to conscientiousness, dependability, and goal-directed behaviour.
- Pressure Regulation relates to emotional stability, self-regulation, and recovery.
- Mutuality & Repair relates to agreeableness, cooperation, trust, and conflict repair.
- Systems Sensemaking and Adaptive Learning relate partly to openness, cognitive complexity, and learning orientation.

This overlap is expected. It supports construct plausibility, but it does not create validation evidence by itself.

### HEXACO Honesty-Humility And Integrity

Trust Stewardship draws partly from integrity, humility, fairness, sincerity, and decision-quality research. It deliberately avoids overly moralised item wording because virtue-heavy items are vulnerable to ceiling effects and social desirability.

The v2 wording focuses on decision behaviour:

- surfacing material risks;
- naming weak evidence;
- considering who could be harmed;
- inviting challenge;
- avoiding negotiable process commitments when goals feel important.

### Professional Assessment Design Lessons

Professional tools are useful because they connect everyday work style, pressure behaviour, values, context, and debriefable language. WorkStyle Compass borrows that general design lesson, but it does not copy proprietary scale names, item language, scoring structures, or public architectures.

The public explanation should emphasise the original Work Operating System model rather than benchmark brands.

### AI-Era Work Practice

Augmented Judgement is not a fixed personality trait. It is a dynamic work-practice domain affected by role, access, company policy, sector, age cohort, tool maturity, and local risk tolerance.

It should be interpreted as:

> how the respondent currently uses AI/digital tools while preserving verification, privacy, quality, and human accountability.

## Pressure Drift

One overuse item per domain is useful for coaching, but it is not enough to measure a separate risk scale.

Use this language:

> A pressure-drift signal is a reflection prompt, not a diagnosis. It suggests where a useful style could become costly under pressure, overuse, or poor context fit.

Avoid language that implies a validated risk score.

## Response Model

V2 keeps a 5-point agreement scale:

| Value | Label |
| ---: | --- |
| 1 | Strongly disagree |
| 2 | Disagree |
| 3 | Neither agree nor disagree |
| 4 | Agree |
| 5 | Strongly agree |

Future validation should test whether frequency wording performs better for some items.

## Scoring Summary

Each domain has:

- 4 core items;
- 1 reverse item;
- 1 overuse item.

Core scale scoring:

1. Score core items raw 1-5.
2. Score reverse items as `6 - raw`.
3. Exclude overuse items from the core score.
4. Average the five scored items.
5. Convert to 0-100.

Descriptive bands:

| Band | Range | Interpretation |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Lower | 0-39 | Less characteristic of the respondent's current work style. |
| Situational | 40-69 | Context-dependent or mixed expression. |
| Strong | 70-100 | Strongly characteristic of the respondent's current work style. |

## Operating-System Composites

| Composite | Formula | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Operational Clarity | Commitment Rhythm + Systems Sensemaking + Augmented Judgement | Turning complexity into usable work progress. |
| Human Coordination | Mobilising Communication + Mutuality & Repair + Trust Stewardship | Creating shared understanding, trust, and commitment. |
| Adaptive Capacity | Adaptive Learning + Pressure Regulation + Change Navigation | Updating, recovering, and moving through uncertainty. |

These composites are descriptive summaries, not norms.

## Results Interpretation

The report should include:

- use-boundary reminder;
- archetype as narrative shorthand, not a fixed type;
- top three domains;
- three operating-system composites;
- nine domain scores;
- pressure-drift signals when triggered;
- current public sample comparison only when threshold is met;
- one 30-day development experiment.

Current public sample comparison must always say it is not a representative norm.

## Assessor / Coach Debrief Protocol

1. Contract the use boundary: this is reflection and development, not judgement.
2. Start with context: ask what work environment the respondent is in now.
3. Validate, then complicate: what feels accurate, and where is it too simple?
4. Explore pressure drift: where could a useful strength become costly?
5. Choose one experiment: what behaviour will be tested in the next 30 days?

Avoid:

- labelling the person as an archetype;
- inferring job suitability;
- making clinical interpretations;
- over-interpreting small score differences;
- treating public sample comparison as norms;
- treating pressure-drift signals as diagnoses.

## Validation Roadmap

### Phase 1: Content Review

- 3-5 occupational psychologists or assessment specialists.
- 5-10 HR/OD practitioners.
- Review construct map, item clarity, relevance, cultural assumptions, and social desirability risk.

### Phase 2: Cognitive Interviews

- 15-25 respondents across role levels and sectors.
- Ask participants what each item means to them.
- Identify ambiguous, double-barrelled, culturally loaded, or role-biased wording.

### Phase 3: Pilot Dataset

Target: n = 300-500.

Analyse:

- item distributions;
- missingness;
- response quality;
- item-total correlations;
- alpha and omega;
- scale intercorrelations;
- reverse-item behaviour;
- pressure-item endorsement rates.

### Phase 4: Factor Structure

- Exploratory factor analysis on one sample.
- Confirmatory factor analysis on a separate sample.
- Test whether the nine domains and three operating-system composites are empirically defensible.

### Phase 5: Validity Evidence

Compare with public-domain and established constructs where appropriate:

- Big Five/IPIP;
- HEXACO Honesty-Humility and Conscientiousness;
- learning orientation;
- psychological safety/team trust proxies;
- self-rated and peer-rated work outcomes.

### Phase 6: Fairness And Invariance

- Differential item functioning where sample size permits.
- Measurement invariance for core groups.
- Review subgroup score distributions.
- Do not publish subgroup claims until sample size and fairness review support them.

## Public Explanation

Use this language:

> WorkStyle Compass is a developmental assessment of how people turn complexity into contribution. It looks at three work operating systems: Operational Clarity, Human Coordination, and Adaptive Capacity. Each domain describes a productive work pattern, a contextual trade-off, and a possible pressure drift. Results are designed for self-reflection, coaching, team conversation, and open research. They are not validated for hiring, promotion, redundancy, compensation, diagnosis, or other high-stakes decisions.
